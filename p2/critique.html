Mangione's on Main

Mangione's website is very clean and features rotating images to showcase the wide array of dining options. I don't have any complaints about this site aside from the oddly sized map at the bottom of the page to help you locate the store.


Manhattan Pizza

Manhattan overall has a very nice site as well. However, the rotating yelp reviews are not helping the page's design. I think it looks quite tacky and is a bit into itself in the wrong ways. Reviews from customers that are not live can definitely feel more curated than anything else.


Pappadeaux

This website is definitely a bit strange, but in a way that I would call stylized. It doesn't falll into the same design pattern that the other websites have been in so far as it doesn't have a clean and small navigation with a giant picture of food. It has a giant picture of food with deals and surely that is a bit different. The style of the page also owns the seafood look which is very effective in my opinion.


Magnolia

The home page of this website fills the entire screen with a picture of the serving counter which does give a great example of what kind of decor to expect their. It establishes it's style very quickly. My issue here is that there seems to be some misleading navigation towards the bottom of the page, pointing you to non existing content.


Kung Fu Tea

I like this page design, but I do believe it could off putting for many due to the large number of things that appear on screen. Deals and offers are nice, but they could stand to be a bit more organized. That said, the other sub pages are a real treat to navigate. They even offer a drink finder on the page that looks and works great on mobile as well.
